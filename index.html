Every build starts with select hardwood slabs — no veneer, no fillers. The species sets the density, the weight, and how the fire walks the grain. Each surface is charred with an open flame, brushed back, and sealed so the figure reads almost three‑dimensional in the right light.

This is a hand‑driven take on shou sugi ban tuned for furniture‑grade work, not siding. Once a slab is gone, that exact grain pattern and flame map is gone with it. Matched sets are planned from the first cut and built to the room they will live in.

This is for people done with flimsy furniture — the ones who want weight, grain, and fire that still looks like wood up close.

Investment & pricing

Built for long money, not fast furniture

Prices follow a simple pattern: we start with the slab and hardware, then add the hours it will take to design, build, and finish the piece the right way. The more complex the layout, joinery, or fire work, the more time it demands. Most clients see a clear, written number up front that reflects both the material on the floor and the hours at the bench.

The ranges below are a starting point for standard builds at a 60–80/hr shop rate. If you’re working with unusual dimensions, specific species, or a space that needs more planning, we talk through that before anything is cut.

Process & care

Fire, grain, and daily use

Surfaces are burned with an open torch, then brushed back to clean grain and sealed with oil or satin topcoats tuned for daily, real‑world use. It is still wood — it will pick up a story over time — but it won’t flake char every time you touch it.

Coffee rings, plates, keys, kids — these pieces are meant to live through regular life, not sit behind velvet rope. If you want the finish pushed toward more “gallery” or more “workhorse,” the build can be biased either way.
