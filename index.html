How It's Made

The Fire Is
The Finish

This is shou sugi ban — an ancient Japanese technique adapted for furniture-grade hardwood. The flame doesn't just finish the surface. It hardens it, seals it, and pulls the grain into relief. The result is permanent. It doesn't chip, fade, or peel.

01
Select the Wood

Solid hardwood slabs only — no particle board, no veneer. The species sets the density, the weight, and how fire walks the grain.

02
Cut & Shape

Dimensions cut to spec, edges shaped by hand. Raw and square or smooth and rounded — every cut is intentional before the torch comes out.

03
Open Flame Finish

A propane torch passes over the surface in controlled passes — charring and hardening. Shou sugi ban adapted by hand. No two pieces look exactly alike.

04
Brush & Seal

Char brushed back to reveal grain depth and contrast. A final oil or satin topcoat protects it for daily real-world use — not a display piece.

Torch Your

This is exactly what happens to every slab that leaves the shop. The fire doesn't just color the wood — it changes it permanently.

Drag the torch across the canvas to char the wood. Crank the heat for deep black char, or keep it low for a light bronze burn. Sand back to pull the grain out of the char — that contrast is what makes every piece unique.

No two passes of the torch look the same. That's not a bug. It's the whole point.

Every Grain
Tells a Story

The deeper the char, the more the grain stands out. What you're seeing is real wood physics — fire contracts the soft grain and leaves the hard grain raised.

Rotate the slab. Look at the grain from different angles. That three-dimensional depth is permanent — it doesn't wash out, fade, or wear flat. The more it's used, the better it looks.

Every piece is inspected and approved by hand before it leaves the shop. If the grain doesn't look right after torching, it goes back under the flame.

Built to Last

How to Keep It
Alive

Torch-finished hardwood is one of the most durable surfaces you can own. Here's what you need to know to keep it that way for decades.

🧽
Daily Cleaning

Damp cloth only. Wipe with the grain, not against it. No harsh chemicals, no abrasive scrubbers — they strip the seal and dull the char. For spills, blot immediately.

🛢️
Annual Re-oiling

Once a year, apply a thin coat of food-safe mineral oil or a hardwax oil (Rubio Monocoat works well). Wipe on, let it soak 20 minutes, buff off the excess. It deepens the grain and refreshes the seal.

☀️
Sun & Heat

Direct, sustained sunlight will lighten the char over years. A UV-blocking finish coat (applied at build time on request) extends the depth. Keep away from heating vents — dry air causes checking in any solid wood.

🪵
How It Ages

Unlike painted or veneered furniture, this piece gets better with time. The char softens slightly over years of use, revealing more grain. Scratches are nearly invisible against the texture. This is wood that's meant to be lived with.

🔥
The Blackline Guarantee Every piece leaves with a satisfaction guarantee. If the finish fails, checks, or peels within the first year under normal use, I'll make it right — no questions, no invoice. I stand behind the work because I'm the only one who did it.
