# Blackline Flameworks
Flame‑finished hardwood, one‑off pieces built by hand in Clarksville, Arkansas.

**One‑person shop · Limited monthly output**

## Hand-Built Slabs, Boards, Tables, & Panels
Every build starts with select hardwood slabs — no veneer, no fillers. The species sets the density, the weight, and how the fire walks the grain. Each surface is charred with an open flame, brushed back, and sealed so the figure reads almost three‑dimensional in the right light.

This is a hand‑driven take on shou sugi ban tuned for furniture‑grade work, not siding. Once a slab is gone, that exact grain pattern and flame map is gone with it. Matched sets are planned from the first cut and built to the room they will live in.

This is for people done with flimsy furniture — the ones who want weight, grain, and fire that still looks like wood up close.

## Flagship Lineup: From Hand to Room‑Scale
Built from the same stock and finish system, scaled from something you can carry to something the room is built around.

### Slab Boards & Wall Pieces
Wall‑grade slab boards, flame‑finished and brushed to pull strong vertical grain. Hangs as art or lays flat as a heavy serving / charcuterie board. Hardware pre‑installed and ready to hang.

### Full‑Scale Furniture
Solid hardwood tops — no joints pretending to be a slab. Torch‑finished surfaces with leveled, sealed sheen: enough protection for daily use, enough texture that it still feels like wood under your hand.

### Coasters, Keys, & Candle Blocks
Small‑run accessory pieces built from the same stock as the big slabs — same torch work, same finish system. Entry point into the line, priced for gifting but built like furniture.

### Panels & Room‑Scale Pieces
Architectural wall panels, benches, and one‑off focal pieces. Start with a photo and rough dimensions of your space; the rest is designed around how you actually live in the room.

## Process & Care
Surfaces are burned with an open torch, then brushed back to clean grain and sealed with oil or satin topcoats tuned for daily, real‑world use. It is still wood — it will pick up a story over time — but it won't flake char every time you touch it.
